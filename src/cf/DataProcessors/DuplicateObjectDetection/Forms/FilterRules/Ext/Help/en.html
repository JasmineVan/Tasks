Designed to filter list items.

You can access this form from the Duplicates search and deletion window by clicking the link in the Filter field.

Entering filter conditions

  • Click Add new item.
  • Select a Field from the list to use as a filter.
  • In the Comparison type column, Select a comparison condition. Depending on the selected list, you can use standard comparison types (Equal, Not equal, Greater than, Less than, etc.), or complex ones:
    • With the In list comparison type, you can define a list of possible values to filter. The Not In List comparison type is an inversion of the previous one.
    • The Contains and Not contains operations are used to filter by a part of the field (a combination of letters).
    • To find out whether a field contains a value, use the Filled and Not filled operations.
  • Specify a Value. Depending on the selected field and comparison type, Select a value from the list, enter a value, or create a value list (used for the In list and Not in list operations).
  • To delete a filter, click Delete.
  • To confirm and go back to the application, click OK.

Grouping conditions

To refine your filter, you can apply multiple conditions simultaneously. The conditions can be reordered by dragging or clicking and .

  • If a filtering rule includes multiple conditions without grouping, logical operation And is applied to such conditions by default.
  • You can apply logical operations And, Or, and Not to the selected conditions. For this purpose, Group types are implemented: And group, Or group, and Not group.
    • To group conditions, select one or several conditions, and then click Group conditions. By default, the conditions are included in And group.
    • You can change the group type if necessary. To do so, double-click the Group, and then open the Group type selection cell.Select a Group type. You can also edit the group type manually.
    • Drag additional conditions to the group, or create new conditions within the group.
    • To cancel grouping, click More - Ungroup.
