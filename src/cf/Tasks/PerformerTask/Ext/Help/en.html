Tasks are used as a vehicle for user communication and business process automation.

To create a task, you can utilize a business process, such as Job.

Task operations

The following lists are used for a variety of task-related operations:

  • My tasks. This is the summary list containing all tasks assigned to the user. It is located on the desktop.
  • To open My tasks, use the corresponding command in the Business processes and tasks section.
  • To open a list of tasks for a business process, use the Tasks command on the navigation panel of the business process.
  • To open a list of tasks created on the basis of a document, use the Tasks command on the document navigation panel.
  • All tasks. This is the summary list containing all tasks assigned to all users. To open All tasks, use the corresponding command in the Business processes and tasks section.

Task notification settings

  • To ensure notifying performers of any new tasks, you need to enable the New performer task notifications scheduled job that sends new task notifications by email on a regular basis. The notifications are sent to the email address specified in the contacts of the user.
  • To set up a scheduled job, go to the Scheduled and background jobs window.

Task monitoring

A variety of reports is available for the purpose of task monitoring:

  • Tasks. A report on tasks assigned to the users.
  • Unassigned tasks. A report on tasks that cannot be completed because of blanklist of roles and assignees.
  • Tasks expiring on date. A report used to monitor own tasks.
  • Overdue tasks. A report on overdue uncompleted tasks.
